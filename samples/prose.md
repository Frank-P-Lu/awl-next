# On Writing Plainly

Good prose is like a windowpane. You look through it
at the idea, not at the glass.

Short sentences carry weight. Long ones, when earned,
unspool a thought across a single breath and then stop.

  Indented lines survive too.
  Tabs and spaces both render.

That is all an editor owes you: get out of the way,
keep the cursor where you left it, and never lie about
what you saved.
