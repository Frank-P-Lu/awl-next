# The Windowpane

Good prose is like a windowpane — you look *through* it
at the idea, not at the glass. awl gets out of the way:
a calm surface, one warm caret, and nothing else asking
for your attention.

Type to insert. The markup recedes; ==the writing stays lit==.
Headings grow by size, not by shouting. Emphasis is a
lean, **weight** is a press, and code rides its own
`monospace grid` when a passage needs it.

---

## A Second Thought

Short sentences carry weight. Long ones, when earned,
unspool a thought across a single breath — and then stop.

That is all an editor owes you: keep the cursor where you
left it, and never lie about what you saved.
